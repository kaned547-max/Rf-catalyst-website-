RFCA's core technology integrates RF plasma generation, impedance control, and advanced catalyst architectures within a unified multi-physics design framework. Digital twin modelling is complete across all six coupled domains. Physical validation is underway in partnership with Oxford University.

The system architecture addresses the fundamental limitations of passive catalyst systems — enabling adaptive, real-time control of emission conversion processes across dynamic operating conditions. Physical hardware validation of the RFCA digital modelling framework has been outsourced to Oxford University. Validation testing is scoped, priced, and actively progressing — establishing independent third-party technical credibility.

RFCA has confirmed Technology Readiness Level 3 — analytical and experimental proof of concept complete. The digital modelling phase is closed. Progression to TRL 4–5 is the active target through Oxford validation and hardware demonstration.
