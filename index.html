Core Architecture

Six Coupled Physics Domains

FLW

Flow

Gas-phase fluid dynamics and velocity field modelling across the reactor geometry. Controls residence time distribution and mixing efficiency.

FLD

Field

RF electromagnetic field propagation, impedance matching, and power coupling to the plasma zone. Determines energy deposition profile.

PLM

Plasma

Non-thermal plasma generation, electron density, and species activation. Provides low-temperature activation of catalytic reactions.

CAT

Catalyst

Surface reaction kinetics, active site dynamics, and conversion efficiency under RF-plasma-assisted conditions across operating temperature ranges.

AGE

Ageing

Catalyst lifetime prediction, fouling mechanisms, sintering, and recovery modelling under real operating cycles and RF regeneration protocols.

CTL

Control

Closed-loop adaptive control across all domains. Real-time impedance matching, plasma density regulation, and catalyst temperature management.

Digital Twin Modelling

Complete six-domain multi-physics digital twin built on open-source simulation infrastructure: OpenFOAM, OpenEMS, ZAPDOS/MOOSE, Cantera, and SciPy/PyMC. All domains modelled and under Oxford independent validation.

High-Entropy Alloy Catalysts

Multi-element alloy catalyst compositions engineered for RF-enhanced performance under non-thermal plasma activation. Designed for reduced precious metal loading without sacrifice to conversion efficiency.

Velocity-Shaped Reactor Geometry

Reactor geometry co-optimised with field distribution and flow velocity profiles. Integrated design couples fluid, electromagnetic, and chemical domains into a unified geometry-chemistry-field solution.

No Known Competitor Integration

Traditional catalyst OEMs, plasma system vendors, and emissions control integrators each address partial domains only. No system currently integrates all six physics domains into a unified design framework.

Company

About RFCA

RF Catalyst Automotive Ltd (RFCA) is a UK-registered deep-technology company developing RF plasma-catalyst integrated reactor systems for emissions reduction, industrial decarbonisation, and advanced propulsion.

The company's technical foundation is a multi-domain physics framework coupling fluid dynamics, RF electromagnetic fields, non-thermal plasma, catalytic surface chemistry, ageing prediction, and closed-loop control into a unified design and modelling system.

No commercial or research system currently integrates all six domains. This integration represents RFCA's primary technical and commercial differentiator.

RFCA is pre-revenue, bootstrapped, and progressing from TRL 3 through structured Oxford University validation toward hardware build and pilot partner engagement.
